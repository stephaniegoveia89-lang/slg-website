The Role, Explained

What is a Chief of Staff?

A Chief of Staff is the strategic operator who sits closest to the CEO — translating vision into execution. Rather than owning one department, a CoS works across the whole organization: managing priorities, driving cross-functional initiatives, and making sure decisions actually turn into action.

Think of it as the difference between having good ideas and having someone whose job is to make sure those ideas get done — on time, by the right people, with the right follow-through.

"You bring the vision. We build the operating system that makes it real."

A Fractional Chief of Staff Typically Owns

  • Weekly leadership briefings and priority management
  • Cross-functional Initiative Execution and Alignment
  • Meeting facilitation and decision documentation
  • Operational Optimization
  • Hiring support and onboarding coordination
  • Financial Workflows
  • Dashboard and Reporting setup and monitoring

And more.

Not Just Another Consultant

A traditional consultant studies your business from the outside. A fractional Chief of Staff becomes part of it — which changes what's actually possible to fix.

The Traditional Consulting Model

  • Parachutes in, delivers a report or slide deck, and moves on to the next client.
  • Learns your org chart — rarely your organization.
  • Applies the same framework and tools used with every other client.
  • Doesn't understand your team's current skill set — and recommends solutions that don't align with the people actually expected to run them.
  • Recommends — then leaves you to implement, manage the change, and troubleshoot alone.
  • Sees one moment in time, then disappears — blind to whatever shifts next.

The SLG Approach

  • We stay embedded until the new system is actually working — accountable for the outcome, not just the recommendation.
  • We learn the players, the relationships, and the sensitivities — the nuance that never shows up in an org chart.
  • We build from what's actually happening inside your business, not a template pulled off the shelf.
  • We do the work: implementation, change management, and the follow-through that makes it stick.
  • We're close enough, long enough, to catch what shifts — a new hire, a strained relationship, a changed priority — and adjust before it becomes a problem.
  • We build the KPIs, metrics, and dashboards to monitor progress and support the change — not just launch it and hope.
